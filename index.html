The Problem We Solve

Healthcare in Bali was broken for visitors

The Gap

Bali welcomes over 6 million international visitors annually. Until BaliDoc, there was no seamless, English-language, digital-first healthcare solution. Tourists navigated language barriers, confusing pharmacy systems, and inaccessible doctors — often in moments of genuine need.

01
Online Doctor Consultations
Video consultations with licensed, English-speaking doctors within minutes — prescriptions issued, questions answered, care delivered.
02
Medication Delivery
Fast, same-day delivery of medications including regulated prescriptions — directly to hotels, villas, and homes across Bali.
03
Local Clinic Partnerships
Trusted partnerships with licensed local clinics and pharmacies, creating an integrated network of care across the island.
04
Innovation-Driven Platform
Continuously integrating the latest healthcare technology to make care more accessible, efficient, and stress-free.

The Founder

Built by someone who understands both worlds

I'm Meilinda Girsang — a serial entrepreneur, double-degree engineer, and MBA graduate who has spent two decades building and scaling businesses across Indonesia and Europe.

My foundation is technical: I hold a Bachelor of Computer Science and a Bachelor of Engineering (Double Major in Industrial Engineering & Information Systems) from BINUS University, graduating with High Merit. I understand how to build systems that scale.

Before founding BaliDoc, I built DePaC Group — a multi-vertical business in food, hospitality, service, and fashion retail — achieving 300% revenue growth in 3 years through digital-first marketing and operations. I also founded DePaC Entrepreneurship Training, running programs for organizations, government institutions, and SMEs across Indonesia.

In 2017, I moved to the Netherlands for my MBA at Maastricht University, specializing in Digital Economy, Big Data & Business Analytics, Industry 4.0, and Digital Transformation. I completed a graduate internship at a high-technology medical equipment company — which planted the seed for what became BaliDoc.

"The Netherlands taught me that the best markets are built on solving real problems at scale. Bali's healthcare gap wasn't just a problem — it was an underserved market of millions."
